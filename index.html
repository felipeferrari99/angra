About ANGRA

ANGRA, a Brazilian progressive power metal band, emerged onto the global metal scene in the early 1990s, leaving an indelible mark with their innovative sound and musical virtuosity. Formed in São Paulo in 1991 by guitarist Rafael Bittencourt, ANGRA quickly gained recognition for their fusion of power metal elements with intricate progressive structures, drawing inspiration from classical music and Brazilian rhythms. The band's debut album, "Angels Cry," released in 1993, showcased their exceptional musicianship and set the stage for a career that would see them become pioneers of the South American metal scene. Over the years, ANGRA has undergone lineup changes, weathered challenges, and evolved their sound, consistently delivering albums that demonstrate both technical prowess and a rich cultural influence. With a legacy spanning decades, ANGRA continues to be a formidable force in the global metal community, celebrated for their enduring musical contributions and their unique blend of diverse influences.
